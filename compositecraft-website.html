CARBON FIBER COMPOSITES

Carbon fiber reinforced polymer (CFRP) is composed of carbon filaments embedded within a resin matrix, typically epoxy. The result is a material that delivers exceptional stiffness, tensile strength, and fatigue resistance at a fraction of the weight of steel or aluminium.

Ideal for products demanding the ultimate strength-to-weight ratio, carbon fiber composites exhibit minimal thermal expansion, superior vibration damping, and an unmistakable woven aesthetic that communicates precision engineering.

FIBERGLASS COMPOSITES

Glass fiber reinforced polymer (GRP), commonly known as fiberglass, is created by combining fine glass fibers with polyester or vinylester resin. This versatile composite offers excellent impact resistance, electrical insulation, and weatherproof durability at a significantly lower cost than carbon fiber.

Fiberglass composites excel in applications requiring complex shapes, UV stability, and long-term exposure to harsh outdoor conditions. The material can be finished in virtually any color or texture — from smooth gloss to natural stone effects.

Manufacturing Process

FROM Concept
TO COMPLETION

Our vertically integrated manufacturing process ensures quality control at every stage — from raw material selection through final finishing and delivery.

DESIGN & ENGINEERING

Every project begins with detailed consultation and CAD modeling. Our engineers analyze structural loads, environmental exposure, and aesthetic requirements to specify the optimal fiber layup schedule, resin system, and manufacturing approach for your product.

01
02

MOLD FABRICATION

Precision molds are CNC-machined or hand-crafted from master plugs. Our mold-making capabilities support complex geometries, undercuts, and multi-part assemblies — ensuring dimensional accuracy and repeatable production quality.

LAMINATION & LAYUP

Carbon fiber or fiberglass fabrics are carefully laid into the mold according to the engineered ply schedule. Resin is applied through hand layup, vacuum infusion, or resin transfer molding (RTM) depending on the product specifications and volume requirements.

03
04

CURING & DEMOLDING

Parts are cured under controlled temperature and pressure conditions. Post-cure heat treatments optimize cross-linking within the resin matrix, maximizing mechanical properties. Parts are then carefully demolded and inspected for dimensional accuracy.

FINISHING & QUALITY CONTROL

Each product undergoes precision trimming, surface preparation, primer application, and final finishing. Our quality assurance process includes visual inspection, dimensional verification, and structural testing to ensure every piece meets our exacting standards before packaging and delivery.

Why Composites

THE COMPOSITE Advantage

Composite materials offer a unique combination of properties that traditional materials simply cannot match. Here's why industries worldwide are choosing composite solutions.

01

SUPERIOR STRENGTH-TO-WEIGHT

Carbon fiber composites deliver five times the strength of steel at roughly one-third the weight. This makes composites ideal for applications where reducing mass improves performance, handling, or installation logistics.

02

CORROSION & WEATHER RESISTANCE

Unlike metal or wood, composite materials do not rust, rot, or degrade when exposed to moisture, salt air, UV radiation, or chemical environments. Products maintain their structural integrity and appearance for decades.

03

DESIGN FREEDOM

The molding process allows us to create virtually any shape — from smooth geometric forms to complex organic curves. Composites can replicate the appearance of stone, metal, wood, or concrete while being a fraction of the weight.

04

THERMAL STABILITY

Carbon fiber composites exhibit very low thermal expansion coefficients, meaning products maintain dimensional stability across extreme temperature fluctuations — critical for outdoor architectural installations and precision components.

05

LONGEVITY & LOW MAINTENANCE

With no susceptibility to biological decay, galvanic corrosion, or fatigue under cyclic loading, composite products typically outlast their metal, wood, and concrete counterparts by a significant margin — with minimal maintenance required.

06

SUSTAINABLE MANUFACTURING

Our production processes are engineered to minimize waste and energy consumption. The extended lifespan of composite products reduces replacement frequency, and many of our fiberglass formulations incorporate recycled content.
